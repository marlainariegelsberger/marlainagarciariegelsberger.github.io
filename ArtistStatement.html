artist statement

My dances are capsules of my and the dancers’ state, at that time in that place. They will never be the same again, nor are they designed to be. Built from scores, scripts, archives of thought, and non-functional tasks, these works aim to relay such state without a performative, refilled, polished filter, to relay ourselves as we are. I work and play collaboratively, aim to do without hierarchy, and ask the performers to bring their identities into the work.

Resume Works

Based in pedestrian gestures and a technical application of form (pulling from my training in ballet, improv, Horton, Cunningham, and Gyrokinesis), the language is recognizable, grounding to the everyday world, yet morph through odd, picturesque and often absurd negotiations of nearness, spatial patterning, and collision of touch. I strive to find a different way of holding our bodies, working against and with habitual pathways, sensation, spontaneity and flow, aiming to incorporate hindsight into the present moment. 


My works incorporate my curiosity of private spaces vs. public spaces and our corresponding orientations in navigating each. Carving a way for audiences to settle in discomfort, dissonance, and yet not isolate themselves from it, but find communal empathy, without eroding the sacredness of a present moment.


There is an attraction for me at this point in my life for such mischievous defiance as fully sharing one’s-self under a pretense of curation. A gesture in reclaiming space for expression of all beings, in all orientations and states of existence, while building in sustainability, accessibility and social/cultural repair for our bodies and generations to come. Growing in hope of mindfully disrupting how ways of embodying the space around ourselves and others program our thoughts for future interactions.
