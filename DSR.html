Arts Administration
and Dance Science Research

A member of the International Association for Dance Medicine and Science (IADMS) since 2014 and attendee of their Annual Conference in 2015, Marlaina has immersed themselves in the interdisciplinary field bridging the science and arts communities through community advocacy and educational engagement. Minoring in Child and Adolescent Mental Health Studies in conjunction with their BFA in Dance at New York University, they were invited to attend the Annual Task Force on Dancer Health Meeting at the 2019 Dance/USA conference. Pre-pandemic, Marlaina explored the developmental relationship to movement through assistant teaching kindergarten at Public School #40, while working as a Research Assistant and Study Navigator on a NEA grant-funded research study through the Community Action Research Lab at Gibney Dance Center, partnered with UMass Amherst and Sanctuary for Families. 

Previously holding positions as the Assistant to the Department Chair and Director of Second Avenue Dance Company at NYU Tisch School of the Arts, Operations Assistant at the spiritual wellness company, Maureen St. Germain & Transformational Enterprises, Inc., and co-founding multiple large scale service projects, Marlaina has worked within both large and small institutions, furthering their specialized skill set in personal relations and detail-oriented project management, whilst expanding their view of wellness and our responsibility to practitioners outside the scope of western ideologies. Such work has further cultivated Marlaina’s desire to be a part of projects that provide the opportunity to analyze, challenge, and reorganize current practices to integrate sustainability, equity, social justice and accessibility at the foundational level, to better the overall wellness and prosperity of the entire community.

Previous Employment: 
"Assistant to the Directors of Second Avenue Dance Company" (2018-2019)

As the Assistant to the Directors of Second Avenue Dance Company (2018-2019) Marlaina was responsible for developing marketing collateral for concerts including programs and posters, concert ticketing and RSVP, publishing monthly newsletters, and managing the company website and social media accounts. Additionally, they coordinated internal and external communication, maintained rehearsal schedules, worked closely with Tisch Dance faculty to research and invite guest artists for master classes, lecture demonstrations, and discussions, and planned receptions and other events for the NYU Alumni Network and prospective students. During this time, Marlaina worked proficiently in Adobe InDesign, Google Drive, Google Docs, Google forms, Q Lab and Microsoft Office.
