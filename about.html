Marlaina Garcia Riegelsberger (she/they) is a queer performance artist based in Queens. A graduate of NYU Tisch Dance, Marlaina has performed with the Scottish Ballet Youth Ensemble at The Joyce Theater, as well as in works by Yin Yue, Christina Robson, and Merce Cunningham. 

Marlaina has guest performed with dance companies such as Backhausdance, MotionTribe, and GoodTrouble Makers in Los Angeles, and abroad with Martz Contemporary Dance Company (Barcelona) and GAUCompany (Berlin). Other freelance work in New York City includes performances at Battery Dance Festival, Dance Gallery Festival (New York/Texas), CPR, ArtsOnSite, 14th Street Y with Brandon Woolf, The Tank NYC, The Issue Project Room, Green Space and the Baryshnikov Arts Center as a part of the Merce Cunningham Centennial. .

During the pandemic, Marlaina co-founded/hosted Harvest, a socially-distant seasonal performance party focusing on the witnessing and gathering of artists in the community

A member of the International Association for Dance Medicine and Science (IADMS) since 2014 and attendee of their Annual Conference in 2015, Marlaina has immersed themselves in the interdisciplinary field bridging the science and arts communities through community advocacy and educational engagement. Minoring in Child and Adolescent Mental Health Studies in conjunction with their BFA in Dance at New York University, they were invited to attend the Annual Task Force on Dancer Health Meeting at the 2019 Dance/USA conference. Pre-pandemic, Marlaina explored the developmental relationship to movement through assistant teaching kindergarten at Public School #40, while working as a Research Assistant and Study Navigator on a NEA grant-funded research study through the Community Action Research Lab at Gibney Dance Center, partnered with UMass Amherst and Sanctuary for Families. 

Previously holding positions as the Assistant to the Department Chair and Director of Second Avenue Dance Company at NYU Tisch School of the Arts, Operations Assistant at the spiritual wellness company, Maureen St. Germain & Transformational Enterprises, Inc., and co-founding multiple large scale service projects, Marlaina has worked within both large and small institutions, furthering their specialized skill set in personal relations and detail-oriented project management, whilst expanding their view of wellness and our responsibility to practitioners outside the scope of western ideologies.
